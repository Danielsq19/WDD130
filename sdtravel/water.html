On The Water

Fishing The water In San Diego is home to many different ane unique species of fish. If you like to catch some awesome live fish, have some fun, and have a peaceful time on the water, then going on a fishing charter is the otion for you. Average price for a charter is $200 per hour, per person. If that is too expensive for you and/or you want to stay ont he main land, then there are plenty of areas on the boardwalks and beaches to cast for fish.

Beaches Discover the best beaches for families and off-leash dogs, beaches coveted by the worlds top surfers and the best beaches for bonfires, bike rides, tide pooling and more. So grab your beach towels, slather on the sunscreen, and get out to explore some of the finest beaches and bays in the world. Some of these beaches include Carlsbad, Del Mar, La Jolla, San Onofre, and many more.

Snorkeling San Diego has a 6,000-acre underwater reserve that is one of the most biodiverse ecosystems in the world. Snorkelers can swim through beautiful schools of fish, including the bright orange Garibaldi, as well as seasonal visits from pods of migrating whales, sharks and dolphins. San Diego has many differnet snorkeling companies that offer daily guided tours in the marine sanctuary. On average, these snorkeling tours cost $80 per person.

Whale Watching Off the coast of San Deigo homes many natural habitats for blue whales. You can take a calm boat ride out into the deep to observe these majestic animals. Tickets cost about $50-$80 per person.
