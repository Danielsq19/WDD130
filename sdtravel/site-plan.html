Content

Home page

San Diego. CA has all the sights, attractions, and culture you could ever need and much more. With how vast and plentiful the city is, planning a trip can be frustrating and overwhelming. San Diego Travel Guide has found all the top rated travel sights in the city so that you can plan your trip easier. This organized travel guide website has have many different types of travel sights with images and descriptions about each one. By scrolling through, you can financially plan for your whole trip because rates will also be included. ATTRACTIONS San Diego Zoo The Zoo is one of the figureheads of attractions here in San Diego. It is one of the largest Zoo's in the world and is well known for its lush, naturalistic habitats and unique animal encounters. It is home to more than 3,700 rare and endangered animals representing approximately 660 species and subspecies and a prominent botanical collection with more than 700,000 plants. The zoo has fun for all ages and all families. Singel day tickets for children ages 3-11, cost $55, and adult tickets cost $55 SeaWorld From swooshing and swooping rides to interactive animal experiences and world-class attractions, See—and learn about—the amazing undersea worlds of animals from around the globe, including penguins, turtles, walruses, beluga whales, and sea lions. Behind the scenes, SeaWorld is also home to marine animals that were injured or orphaned on Southern California beaches, and are being nursed or rehabilitated before they can be returned to the wild. Tickets are $99 but kids under 3 enter free. USS Midway The USS Midway was the longest-serving aircraft carrier in the 20th century. Named after the climactic Battle of Midway of June 1942, Midway was built in only 17 months, but missed World War II by one week when commissioned on September 10, 1945. Midway was the first in a three-ship class of large carriers that featured an armored flight deck and a powerful air group of 120 planes. It is now decomissioned and now serves as a fun and interactive museum. You can expirience the real life planes, machines, weapons, and working stations that were used when it was in active duty. Kids, (ages 6-12), tickets cost $18 and adult tickets cost $26. Balboa Park Balboa Park is a cultural oasis that includes 17 museums, gardens galore and the world-famous San Diego Zoo. At 1,200 acres, Balboa Park is one of North Americas most iconic urban parks and a must see on any visit to San Diego. Located just blocks from downtown San Diego hotels, the park has a rich history reflected through its stunning architecture, thought-provoking exhibits installations, and cultural events throughout the year. Visiting the grounds is free ,but for the museums, children tickets cost $35 and adult tickets cost $56

On Land

ON LAND Hiking San Diego may conjure images of sandy beaches and curling surf, but the sprawling county also boasts unmatched biodiversity. Venture outside the city center to find deserts marked by otherworldly badlands, coniferous forests, and 259 named mountainsstretching thousands of feet into the sky. The best way to experience these natural wonders? On foot, of course. Golfing Perfect weather, challenging course, and incredible settings make San Diego County a golfers dream destination. It is not surprising that many of the world’s leading golf companies—Titleist, TaylorMade, Callaway—have offices or headquarters here.While you’ll find great golf throughout the county, two area courses took honors in Golf Digests most recent rankings of the greatest public courses in the U.S. Maderas Golf Club and Torrey Pines Golf Course are described as “some of the prettiest golf course sites in America.” Food As a very diverse place with many different culture influences to influence it, San Diego is a home to many different styles of good food. Specifically, we are known for our California,Woodfired style, pizza, south-east asian specialties, mexican food, and seafood. There are so many different restaurants to choose from, with thousands of very tasty options. You can find everything from street vendors to 5 star steakhouses. The Gliderport You can soar in the sky across the clear ocean water at the Torrey Pines Gliderport. Sitting on top of the 100 ft beach cliffs, there you can expirience how to fly by choosing either paragliding or hang gliding. You can see beautiful ocean views and feel the ocean breeze on your face as you fly amongst the seagulls. This expirience costs $175 for paragliding and $225 for hang gliding

On the Water

Fishing The water In San Diego is home to many different ane unique species of fish. If you like to catch some awesome live fish, have some fun, and have a peaceful time on the water, then going on a fishing charter is the otion for you. Average price for a charter is $200 per hour, per person. If that is too expensive for you and/or you want to stay ont he main land, then there are plenty of areas on the boardwalks and beaches to cast for fish. Beaches Discover the best beaches for families and off-leash dogs, beaches coveted by the worlds top surfers and the best beaches for bonfires, bike rides, tide pooling and more. So grab your beach towels, slather on the sunscreen, and get out to explore some of the finest beaches and bays in the world. Some of these beaches include Carlsbad, Del Mar, La Jolla, San Onofre, and many more. Snorkeling San Diego has a 6,000-acre underwater reserve that is one of the most biodiverse ecosystems in the world. Snorkelers can swim through beautiful schools of fish, including the bright orange Garibaldi, as well as seasonal visits from pods of migrating whales, sharks and dolphins. San Diego has many differnet snorkeling companies that offer daily guided tours in the marine sanctuary. On average, these snorkeling tours cost $80 per person. Whale Watching Off the coast of San Deigo homes many natural habitats for blue whales. You can take a calm boat ride out into the deep to observe these majestic animals. Tickets cost about $50-$80 per person.
