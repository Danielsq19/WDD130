On Land

Hiking San Diego may conjure images of sandy beaches and curling surf, but the sprawling county also boasts unmatched biodiversity. Venture outside the city center to find deserts marked by otherworldly badlands, coniferous forests, and 259 named mountainsstretching thousands of feet into the sky. The best way to experience these natural wonders? On foot, of course!

Golfing Perfect weather, challenging course, and incredible settings make San Diego County a golfers dream destination. It is not surprising that many of the world’s leading golf companies—Titleist, TaylorMade, Callaway—have offices or headquarters here.While you’ll find great golf throughout the county, two area courses took honors in Golf Digests most recent rankings of the greatest public courses in the U.S. Maderas Golf Club and Torrey Pines Golf Course are described as “some of the prettiest golf course sites in America.”

Food As a very diverse place with many different culture influences to influence it, San Diego is a home to many different styles of good food. Specifically, we are known for our California,Woodfired style, pizza, south-east asian specialties, mexican food, and seafood. There are so many different restaurants to choose from, with thousands of very tasty options. You can find everything from street vendors selling tacos to 5 star steakhouses.

The Gliderport You can soar in the sky across the clear ocean water at the Torrey Pines Gliderport. Sitting on top of the 100 ft beach cliffs, there you can expirience how to fly by choosing either paragliding or hang gliding. You can see beautiful ocean views and feel the ocean breeze on your face as you fly amongst the seagulls. This expirience costs $175 for paragliding and $225 for hang gliding
